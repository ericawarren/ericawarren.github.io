U.S. Engagement in the Arts

Frequency of instruction in the arts in U.S. elementary schools, 2009-2010.

United States Department of Education. Institute of Education Sciences. National Center for Education Statistics. Fast Response Survey System (FRSS): Arts Education Surveys of Elementary School Teachers, 2009-2010.

Most students are taught Music and, to a lesser extent, Visual Arts, once or twice a week, but instruction in Dance and Drama is rare.

Does poverty-level affect amount of arts offered in elementary school?

United States Department of Education. Institute of Education Sciences. National Center for Education Statistics. Fast Response Survey System (FRSS): Arts Education Surveys of Elementary School Teachers, 2009-2010.

As the percentage of students eligible for free or reduced lunch goes down, the amount of Visual Arts instruction goes up, though the trend is slight.

Does poverty-level affect amount of arts offered in elementary school?

United States Department of Education. Institute of Education Sciences. National Center for Education Statistics. Fast Response Survey System (FRSS): Arts Education Surveys of Elementary School Teachers, 2009-2010.

There is less of a trend with Music than there is with Visual Arts.

Does poverty-level affect adequacy of supplies available for visual arts instruction in elementary school?

Source: United States Department of Education. Institute of Education Sciences. National Center for Education Statistics. Fast Response Survey System (FRSS): Arts Education Surveys of Elementary School Teachers, 2009-2010.

As poverty increases, support offered for visual arts supplies decreases.

Does poverty-level affect adequacy of supplies available for music instruction in elementary school?

United States Department of Education. Institute of Education Sciences. National Center for Education Statistics. Fast Response Survey System (FRSS): Arts Education Surveys of Elementary School Teachers, 2009-2010.

As poverty increases, support offered for instruments used by students decreases.

Per Capita State Legislative Arts Appropriation From 1970 to 2014

Source: TK

As the population grows, we would expect arts funding to grow as well. Coincidentally, there appears to be a linear relationship from 1970 to 1991 or even 2001. However there is a sharp decline in state arts appropriations that begins in 2002 and bottoms out in 2012 leaving arts appropriations at the level we might expect in the 1990s.
